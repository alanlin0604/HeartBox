Summary of WHO Mental Health Action Plan 2013–2030

The WHO Mental Health Action Plan provides a comprehensive framework for promoting mental well-being worldwide. Key principles and recommendations include:

1. Universal Health Coverage for Mental Health
- Mental health services should be integrated into general health care systems.
- Access to mental health care should be available to all, regardless of socioeconomic status.
- Community-based mental health services should be prioritized over institutional care.

2. Prevention and Promotion
- Promote mental well-being across the lifespan through school-based programs, workplace wellness initiatives, and community engagement.
- Address social determinants of mental health: poverty, violence, discrimination, and social isolation.
- Implement early intervention programs for children and adolescents at risk.

3. Evidence-Based Strategies
- Use proven psychological interventions such as cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) and interpersonal therapy.
- Train non-specialist health workers to deliver basic mental health care in primary care settings.
- Strengthen mental health information systems for better planning and monitoring.

4. Human Rights and Empowerment
- Respect the rights and dignity of people with mental health conditions.
- Promote recovery-oriented approaches that empower individuals.
- Combat stigma and discrimination through education and awareness campaigns.

5. Self-Care Recommendations
- Maintain regular physical activity (at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week).
- Ensure adequate sleep (7–9 hours for adults).
- Build and maintain supportive social connections.
- Practice stress management techniques such as mindfulness, deep breathing, and relaxation exercises.
- Limit alcohol consumption and avoid substance abuse.
- Seek professional help when experiencing persistent emotional distress.
